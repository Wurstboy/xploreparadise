the mission

EARTH: Our planet, our home. A complex biosphere full of natural wonders. The Okavango Delta in Botswana is one of them. One of the last places on Earth where you’ll find an ordinary ecosystem with a huge diversity of flora and fauna. This website will show you the amazing beauty and uniqueness of the Delta and provides information, facts and above all awareness about how important it is to protect this paradise.


Join an unforgettable journey, an amazing adventure and explore one of the seven natural wonders of Africa.

Africa's last eden

Embedded in the Kalahari basin and formed through tectonic faults, the fan-shaped Okavango Delta is located in the northern part of Botswana. At more than 20,000 square kilometres, the Okavango Delta is the world’s largest inland delta and one of the largest and most fauna-rich wetlands in Africa. Every year, after a long period of drought, a seasonal flood created by the rains in the Angolan highlands slowly snakes down the Okavango River, spreads into the many channels and lagoons of the delta and brings it back to life. The delta is a unique sanctuary and home to a huge variety of wildlife, including over 400 species of birds, over 70 species of fish and over 200,000 mammals.

About a third of the Delta is under conservation, including the oldest nature reserve in Botswana, the Moremi Wildlife Reserve. The Okavango Delta is a part of the Kavango-Zambezi Transfrontier Conservation Area transnational reserve, which spans five nations: Angola, Botswana, Namibia, Zambia and Zimbabwe. Since 2014, the Okavango Delta has also been a UNESCO World Heritage site.

THE INITIATOR

As a young boy, Christoph Tänzer began his deep love of exploration, adventure and nature, dreaming about foreign countries, intriguing places and different cultures. More than 30 years later, these dreams came true for the German creative director, photographer and filmmaker. His passion developed into an idea: Winning people’s understanding through photography and film to protect our planet.

“I tell stories by capturing moments that show the beauty of our planet and the variety of its wildlife, people and cultures. If people understand that some of these moments, places, animals or people will be rare or gone in a couple of years, it’s a start. If they decide to change something, it will make a difference and my mission will have been successful.”
